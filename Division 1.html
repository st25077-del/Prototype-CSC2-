The future of the mod

Main goal

Our goal with this project is to explore what the game might have looked and felt like during its early stages of development. Much of our inspiration comes from the book Raising the Bar, early concept art, and information from prototype builds that revealed many darker and more experimental ideas for the game’s world.

Division 1

When working on Division 1, we focused heavily on recreating the atmosphere that appeared in those early concepts. The environments are designed to feel more industrial, harsh, and oppressive than the final version of Half-Life 2.

Large concrete structures, rusted machinery, dim lighting, and foggy city streets help create a colder and more dystopian setting. We also experimented with level layouts and visual elements that reflect how Valve originally imagined the world during development.

Environments

One of the first things we focused on was environment design. In early builds, City 17 and its surrounding areas had a harsher, more oppressive tone. Buildings were massive and angular, streets were narrow and maze-like, and the lighting was deliberately dim and foggy to give a sense of weight and decay. To capture that, we created levels with large concrete and metal structures, rusted machinery, and scattered debris, emphasizing verticality and claustrophobia.

Atmosphere

In Division 1, We aimed to make every space feel alive and purposeful, even if it’s abandoned or destroyed, echoing Valve’s experimental vision. We also focused on ensuring that every room told part of the story—even if the player doesn’t read every note, the environment itself communicates the narrative. Fog, shadows, and muted color palettes enhance the feeling of isolation and foreboding.

Gameplay

Throughout Division 1, Players encounter unusual obstacles, environmental puzzles, and alternate pathways that were designed to make them think creatively. ALso, Combat gameplay were designed to test timing and strategy rather than brute force.
